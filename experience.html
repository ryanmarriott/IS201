Experience

All of My Previous Work Experience

On top of all my academic studies, I have been blessed with opportunities to be able to work at two different jobs and be a student-athlete.

  1. Quick Quack

    My first job was at Quick Quack Car Wash. At Quick Quack, we were tracked on different stats to see how well we were performing. Being a competitive person, I wanted to be the best employee there. This entailed me to pick up on some pretty important life skills.

    • In the business world, don't accept no as an answer
    • Your attitude reflects on others
    • The most important work is the work you don't want to do
    • The same pitch doesn't work on everyone
    • The amount of effort you put in reflects on the outcome you receive

  2. Jimmy Johns

    While fulfilling my dreams of being a college football player, I needed to find a job to be able to pay my rent, groceries, gas, and other bills. I was lucky to find a job at Jimmy Johns and I gained very valuable knowledge from working in fast food.

    • Don't question yourself and just complete the task as you know how to do it
    • Find work to do during slow hours, so when it is busy your life is easier
    • Mistakes happen, find a way to fix them
    • Let your coworkers help you
    • The more skills you have, the more useful you are

  3. Student-Athlete

    I participated on my high school's football and track team, as well as having the opportunity to move on to the next level and play football while taking college courses. While trying to balance time consuming sports and countless hours of studying, I have learned a couple important skills:

    • Time management
    • Prioritizing work that needs to be done first
    • Completing assignments in a busy schedule
    • Scheduling my work throughout the week
    • Balancing other commitments with my work
